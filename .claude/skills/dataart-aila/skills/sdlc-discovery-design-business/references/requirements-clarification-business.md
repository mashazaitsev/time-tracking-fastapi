# Business Requirements Clarification Skill

## Overview

Business-focused requirements clarification emphasizing stakeholder needs, business objectives, and value proposition.

## Business Dimension Focus

### Stakeholder-Centric Questions
- Who are the primary stakeholders and decision makers?
- What business problem are we solving?
- What is the expected business value or ROI?
- Who will be the end users of this solution?
- What are the success criteria from a business perspective?

### Business Context Areas
- **Business Objectives**: Strategic goals and KPIs
- **Stakeholder Analysis**: Primary, secondary, and tertiary stakeholders
- **Value Proposition**: Expected benefits and outcomes
- **Business Constraints**: Budget, timeline, regulatory requirements
- **Success Metrics**: How success will be measured
- **Risk Assessment**: Business risks and mitigation strategies

### Question Categories

**Strategic Alignment:**
- How does this project align with organizational strategy?
- What business outcomes are expected?
- What happens if we don't implement this solution?

**Stakeholder Requirements:**
- What are the needs of each stakeholder group?
- How will different stakeholders interact with the solution?
- What are the approval and decision-making processes?

**Business Process Impact:**
- What current business processes will be affected?
- How will workflows change?
- What training or change management is needed?

**Value and ROI:**
- What is the expected return on investment?
- How will benefits be measured and tracked?
- What is the timeline for realizing benefits?

## Business Requirements Template

When documenting requirements, structure around:

1. **Business Context**
   - Problem statement
   - Strategic alignment
   - Stakeholder landscape

2. **Business Requirements**
   - Functional requirements from business perspective
   - Business rules and constraints
   - Compliance and regulatory requirements

3. **Success Criteria**
   - Business metrics and KPIs
   - Acceptance criteria
   - Definition of done from business perspective

4. **Stakeholder Analysis**
   - Primary stakeholders and their needs
   - Secondary stakeholders and their interests
   - Decision makers and approval processes
